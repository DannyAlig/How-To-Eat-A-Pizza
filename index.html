How to Eat a Pizza  

Introduction to Eating Pizza  

Pizza is delicious, and so, there are many people around the world who demand pizza of every variety. The modern lover of pizza has a ton of topping options at their fingertips.

   What is pizza? - Pizza on Wikipedia

Pizza is a baked wonder. It's dough spread out thin, covered in a flavorful tomato sauce, topped with cheese and toppings, then baked in a hot oven.

    Some topping schemes:

  • Pepperoni (the classic)
  • Wild forest mushroom
  • BBQ Chicken and caramelized onion
  • Granny Smith apple, smoked gouda and walnut
  • Pineapple Jalape√±o

Given the delectable possibilities, it's essential to know how to procure and consume pizza properly for maximum enjoyment. Continue on to find out how.

Pizza + Any Day = Awesomeness

Acquiring Pizza  

The first step to enjoying pizza is acquiring the pizza. Pizza can be either prepared at home (frozen, take-n-bake, or from scratch) or purchased from a pizzeria or restaurant.

Most grocery stores will have frozen pizzas in their freezer section which can be baked at home. Read the instructions for temperature and time to bake.

If you're not making the pizza at home, do an online search for pizza restaurants in your area, and decide on where you would like to source the pizza.

At this point, if you are doing take out or delivery, call the restaurant or order online. If you are eating at a restaurant, go there and peruse the menu, and order the pizza of your choice.

    Places to source a pizza:

  • Make it at home using this simple recipe
  • Grocery store freezer section
  • Delivery/Takeout from Pizzeria
  • Dining in at a restaurant

Cutting the Pizza  

If you're eating at a restaurant or got delivery, it is likely they have cut the pizza for you. It's now time to choose your slices!
 Next Step

If you're having a home baked pizza and it's done baking, it's now time to cut the pizza. It's recommended to let your pizza cool for a minute or two if it just came out of the oven. Use a pizza cutter wheel or knife to cut the slice pattern you prefer. Two common forms are the triangular slice 'wagon wheel' and the 'square cut' of rectangular sliceage.

L: Wagon Wheel Cut,   R: Square Cut

Place the pizza on a steady cutting surface and use a fair amount of pressure with your cutter to cut fully through the crust.

 It's best to let a home-baked pizza cool for a few minutes before cutting.

Slice Selection and Plate Loading  

Assess the pizza and slices which are available.

 Choose the slices you prefer.

    Some slice selection considerations:

  • Crust-to-topping ratio
  • Topping concentration on a particular slice
  • Plate capacity

 Load the slice(s) onto your plate or slice holding device of choice.

 PRO TIP: If you don't have plates available, you can tear the pizza box and create your own impromptu eating platforms for you and your friends!

 To some people, the crust-less interior slices of a square-cut pizza are ultimate.

Final Topping Additions  

Finishing toppings can take a pizza from delicious to next-level delicious. Experiment and find out what you like!

 Add your finishing toppings if desired, sprinkling evenly over slice to desired level.

    Common finishing toppings:

  • Parmesan cheese
  • Red pepper flakes 
  • Italian herb seasoning

Dipping/Drizzling Sauces:

 At this point, collect any additional dipping sauces or condiments as desired and place them in the eating environment. Ranch dressing, marinara, Sriracha, or honey for finishing the crust are commonly employed. All are delicious!

Solid pizza + Finishing toppings = Uber pizza  

How bout now?

Eating and Appreciating  

It is now time to enjoy the pizza. The main event!

    How to Enjoy a Slice of Pizza:

  1. Grab the slice. 
  2. Pause for a moment, appreciating the slice for all that it is. Notice the perfectly browned caramelization of the onions, the wafting aroma of the bread and delectable toppings, the toasted cheese on the periphery of the crust and topping border.
  3. Put the pizza to your mouth, take a bite and enjoy every aspect of it's flavors, textures, and overall pizza-ey goodness. Mindfully appreciate the individual toppings, sensing their flavor and textures.
  4. When you get to the crust, eat the crust as is as a pizza purist, or get adventurous with dipping the crust in a sauce like ranch or drizzling it with honey.
  5. When you come to the end of a slice, feel your appreciation for how delicious and awesome that was.
  6. Repeat as desired. (While the pizza lasts!)

You have completed the pizza process, congratulations! There is still much pizza frontier to explore. The future is very bright should you continue to advance with a pioneering spirit. Slices up!
