How to Make a Pizza  

Here's a simple recipe for making delicious homemade pizza. It takes about 25 minutes to make and bake the pizza once your dough is ready.

If you prefer to buy the dough pre-made, just skip to the assembly section, but making the dough yourself is actually really easy!

Makes: Two 12-inch pizzas
Equipment: a large pan or pizza stone, oven
 Actual Work Time Required: ~20 minutes
 Total time if making dough: ~2 hours
 Assembly + Baking only: ~25 minutes

 Ingredients
 Dough
 Assembly and Baking

1. Gather Ingredients:

    Dough:

  • 1 ⅓ cups warm water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 packet (~ 2 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 ¾ cups all purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt

    • Rest of pizza:

  • 1 jar pizza sauce (or pesto, ranch...)
  • 1 package/block firm mozzarella cheese
  • All toppings you prefer:
    • Vegetables
    • Proteins/Meats
    • Spices/Seasonings
  • Little bit of cornmeal

2. Make the Dough:

a. Stir the 1 Tbsp sugar into the 1 ⅓ cups warm water.

b. Sprinkle the yeast into the warm water and let sit for 10 minutes.

c. Meanwhile, in a large bowl, whisk together the 3 ¾ cups flour and 1 ½ tsp salt. Make a depression in the middle of the mixture.

d. Add the 2 Tbsp olive oil to the yeast after the 10 minutes and stir. Now dump this into the flour mixture.

e. Use a sturdy spoon to mix everything up for about 20 seconds and create a sticky dough.

f. Flour a surface like a cutting board or the counter top and take the sticky dough and begin working it and kneading it, 4 or 5 minutes, to really mix it well and prepare it for rising. Just keep doubling it and pushing it back into itself, it will become cohesive and softer. Work in a little more flour if the dough is wet.

g. Divide the dough into two even balls and work each a little more to smooth it out. Now add a little drizzle of olive oil to two medium bowls, add a dough ball to each and turn them around in the oil to coat. Add plastic wrap or a lid over each bowl and let them sit in a warm place for 1 hour 20 minutes. Now's a good time to slice toppings and grate cheese for later.

h. After the dough has risen, take one of the expanded dough balls and work it gently in your hands or on a counter top, letting it stretch thinner until it's about 10 inches across. Be mindful not to let it get too thin or tear, but if it does just work it back together on a flat surface. Let it rest for 5 minutes.

 Repeat with the other dough when you're ready to make the second pizza 

i. Sprinkle a little bit of cornmeal around on your pan, which will help the pizza bake and remove from the pan better. Put your rested dough on the pan and keep working it thinner by pressing it out with your hands until it's the desired size. Pinch the dough to make the crust line. You're now ready to add toppings and bake!

3. Assemble the pizza and bake:


 Begin preheating the oven to 450℉ / 230℃   

 If you're using pre-made dough, at this point work the dough a little bit to warm it up, sprinkle some cornmeal on your baking pan, and work the dough into the pizza size and shape you'd like.

a. Slice toppings thin and grate cheese.

b. Spread pizza sauce on dough.

c. Sprinkle cheese.

d. Add toppings. Top everything off with a little more cheese or some seasonings like Italian herb blend or garlic powder.

e. Bake! 10-15 minutes in lower part of preheated oven. Check on your pizza after 10 minutes, it's done when the cheese is golden-brown and the crust begins to brown slightly. Thinner crusts are done earlier. Remove from oven and set to cool for at least a couple minutes.

f. Slice and enjoy! On a sturdy surface, use a pizza wheel cutter or knife to slice the pizza in square cut or wagon wheel configuration, or in a pattern of your own creative design. Add finishing toppings and appreciate the pizza you just made.
